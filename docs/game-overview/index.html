Slopworks

Post-apocalyptic base-building survival with real BIM-modeled buildings, Satisfactory-style automation, and co-op defense.

World structureThree connected spaces

The game runs across three separate loaded scenes. Players transition between them — no seamless streaming required.

Core loopScout → Breach → Clear → Restore → Connect → Automate → Defend → Expand

Each cycle expands your territory and escalates the threat. Expansion pace is player-controlled — push faster for more resources, but face tougher waves.

Resource pipelineRaw materials → processing → final products

Resources flow through a distributed production network. Each reclaimed building is a processing node. Your home base does final assembly.

Defense systemThreat meter drives escalation

Expanding your territory raises the threat meter. Higher threat means tougher, more frequent waves from multiple directions. Your factory layout becomes your defense perimeter.

ProgressionThe Tower — repeatable combat gauntlet

A BIM-modeled skyscraper at home base. Explore floors, fight enemies, collect rare materials and key fragments. Extract to bank your loot — or die and lose it all. The only source of factory modifiers, blueprints, and rare crafting materials.

BIM buildings

Real Revit-modeled interiors. Each run loads a random building from the pool. Offices, mechanical rooms, stairwells — real architecture, not procedural.

Floor chunks

Floors load via elevator transitions. Most are single-floor. Some are multi-floor sections (atriums, lobbies) with internal stairs. Boss is always the top floor.

Risk vs reward

All loot is carried — including key fragments. Die and you lose everything found this run. Only extracting at the lobby banks your finds permanently.

Non-automatable

Tower rewards can only be earned by fighting. No belts, no supply lines, no passive income. The one system that forces the player to pick up the gun.

Technical architectureImplementation overview

Engine

Unity 6 with URP. Three separate scenes (home base, buildings, overworld). No seamless streaming.

Networking

FishNet + FishySteamworks. Server-authoritative simulation. Clients display synced state via SyncVar/SyncList.

Simulation

Fixed-tick factory sim (16-20ms). All logic in plain C# classes (D-004 pattern). MonoBehaviours are thin wrappers.

AI

NPBehave behavior trees. Pack-coordinated fauna with shared blackboards, morale, flanking, and cover-seeking.

BIM pipeline

Revit/Navisworks models exported to FBX/IFC, imported into Unity. Automated mesh cleanup, MEP identification, NavMesh bake.

Multiplayer

1-4 players, drop-in/drop-out co-op. Persistent world. Player inventories owner-visible only. Storage/factory state shared.
