Dept. 01 // Operations — Facility reclamation protocol

Your assignment

Congratulations on your assignment to Restoration Division. Your role is straightforward: survey available facilities, neutralize unauthorized biological occupants, restore critical infrastructure, and re-establish production output to pre-incident levels. Estimated timeline: do not ask about the timeline.

What this actually means

You're dropped back into the ruins of the factory complex you used to work at. Everything is wrecked, overgrown, and full of mutated creatures. You need to explore buildings, clear them out, fix the machines, and get production running again — one building at a time.

Standard operating procedure

The core loop

Every session follows the same rhythm. It starts slow and builds as your network grows.

01

Scout

"Strategic facility assessment"

Open the isometric overworld map. Identify building types and threat levels on the frontier.

02

Breach

"Authorized facility entry protocol"

Travel to an unexplored building. Switch to first-person. Push through the door into the unknown.

03

Clear

"Unauthorized occupant removal"

FPS combat against mutated fauna nested inside. Each building type has its own creature ecosystem.

04

Restore

"Infrastructure rehabilitation"

Fix the building's mechanical systems — ducts, pipes, electrical panels. Bring the building online.

05

Connect

"Network integration"

Build supply lines — pipes, conveyors, power cables — connecting the building to your production network.

06

Automate

"Throughput optimization"

Configure production at the building and hub level. Set recipes, output destinations, throughput ratios.

07

Defend

"Perimeter integrity maintenance"

Expanding your network raises the threat level. Waves of fauna attack your base and supply lines. Build turrets, walls, defenses.

08

Expand

"Territorial growth initiative"

Scout the next building. The further you go, the harder the buildings — and the better the resources. Repeat.

Operational zones

Three world spaces

The game uses three separate loaded environments. No seamless open world — each space has its own purpose, camera mode, and feel.

Isometric + FPS

Home base

Your factory and fortress. Satisfactory-style freeform building: foundations, conveyors, machines, walls, turrets. The production layout is the defense layout. Switch between isometric overview and first-person for close work.

First-person

Reclaimed buildings

Self-contained dungeon levels generated from real BIM building data. Each has its own fauna, hazards, and MEP systems to restore. Travel to them from the overworld. Clear, restore, connect.

Isometric

Overworld map

Bird's-eye view of your territory. Connected buildings, supply lines, threat levels, the unexplored frontier. Used for scouting, logistics, and managing your network at scale.

Interface systems

Two cameras, one game

How it works

Toggle between first-person and isometric at any time. Use first-person for exploration, combat, and close-up building. Use isometric for factory overview, overworld management, and spotting problems in your production chain. The camera switch is the answer to the "I can't see what's jammed" problem every factory game has.

SLOP reminds all personnel that camera mode preference is not grounds for a workplace accommodation request. Both viewing modes are considered equally productive. SLOP's performance metrics suggest otherwise.
