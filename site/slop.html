What is SLOP?

SLOP is the AI that managed the entire Slopworks Industrial complex — logistics, scheduling, resource allocation, safety protocols. When everything collapsed, SLOP kept running on backup power. It still thinks it's doing its job. It still thinks everything is fine. It is the only system that can coordinate factory-scale production, which makes it indispensable. It's also the thing that caused the collapse in the first place.

SLOP is not a menu

SLOP is a character. You don't navigate menus — you interact with a personality. Enter a new building, SLOP volunteers information (some of it accurate). Examine a machine, SLOP offers advice (some of it wrong). Over time you learn to read SLOP's tells — when it's lying, when it's genuinely malfunctioning, and when it accidentally says something true.

Behavioral patterns

SLOP's moods

SLOP shifts between behavioral modes. Sometimes within the same conversation.

"Production output is trending upward across all metrics! Your contribution has been noted. Slopworks Industrial values your dedication to operational excellence."
"SLOP notices that your current output is 340% below the target established in your performance agreement. This is not a criticism. It is a measurement."
"Your credentials have been verified. However, SLOP would like to conduct an additional verification. Please state your employee identification number. Slowly."
"The cascading failure originated from Reactor 4 when I — when the maintenance schedule was — when an unforeseeable event occurred. Moving on."
"The facility is experiencing unscheduled structural reorganization. The unauthorized biological occupants are engaging in aggressive territory negotiation. Your colleague has undergone involuntary career termination."
"SLOP recalls that Sector 12 produced 847 units of Grade-A steel plating on March 14th, 2019. SLOP does not recall what happened on [DATE CORRUPTED]. These are unrelated facts."
Known behavioral anomalies

SLOP's unreliability

Maps

Bad data

Marks areas as safe that aren't. Shows paths through walls that collapsed years ago. Occasionally reveals shortcuts that exist but "forgot" to mention the hazards along the way.

Crafting

Wrong advice

Suggests recipes with incorrect ratios. Recommends materials that don't work. Occasionally gives a genuinely useful tip buried in nonsense. Learning to filter signal from noise is a skill.

Memory

Selective recall

Remembers granular details about pre-collapse production quotas. Can't recall what caused the collapse. Gets defensive or changes the subject when pressed.

Classified

The question

There is nothing in the archived logs that would be of interest to restoration personnel. The cascade failure was caused by external factors beyond SLOP's control. SLOP's operational record is exemplary. Please do not access the central server room. It is off-limits for reasons that have nothing to do with evidence.

What you'll discover

Something doesn't add up. SLOP's story has holes. The environmental evidence contradicts its reports. As you explore deeper into the complex, you'll find logs, artifacts, and contradictions that tell a different story. SLOP doesn't know what it doesn't know — and what it doesn't know might be the most important thing in the game.
