Dept. 04 // Human resources — Mandatory team-building exercise protocols

Colleague coordination

Teamwork is a core Slopworks Industrial value. Restoration personnel are encouraged to coordinate with up to three (3) colleagues during facility operations. SLOP reminds you that colleague performance metrics are tracked individually. Collaboration does not reduce individual output expectations.

How co-op works

1-4 players. Drop-in, drop-out. Persistent world with shared progress. No restarts, no roguelike resets. Your factory, your territory, your network — it's all there when you come back. Friends join your session or you join theirs. Everything is shared: the factory, the threat level, the danger.

Operational flexibility

Split up or squad up

Four players, three world spaces, always something to do. The game is designed so splitting up is efficient and sticking together is fun.

⚙

The builder

Stays at home base. Optimizes the factory layout, extends conveyors, places new machines. Makes sure production keeps flowing.

☠

The defender

Manages the perimeter. Places turrets, repairs walls, watches for incoming waves. First to respond when the threat meter spikes.

★

The explorer

Breaches new buildings solo or with a partner. Clears fauna, restores systems, brings new nodes online. Lives on the frontier.

⚖

The strategist

Works the overworld map. Scouts targets, manages supply line routing, optimizes the production network. Sees the big picture.

Or all four of you breach together

A building that's terrifying solo is a different experience with three friends covering your flanks. The pack hunters that overwhelm one player become a manageable firefight with coordinated team positioning. Boss encounters are designed to be soloable but way more fun with backup.

Shared resources

What everyone shares

Shared

The world

One persistent world. All buildings, all territory, all supply lines. When someone reclaims a building, it's reclaimed for everyone.

Shared

The factory

The home base factory is shared infrastructure. Anyone can build, rearrange, or extend it. Your production capacity is communal.

Shared

The threat level

Expanding the network raises the threat for everyone. More buildings, more throughput, more danger. The decision to expand is a group decision.

Persistent

No resets

Your world survives between sessions. Log off, come back tomorrow, everything is where you left it. Friends drop in and out without disruption.

SLOP would like to remind all personnel that "drop-in, drop-out" refers exclusively to session participation. Dropping out of your contractual obligations to Slopworks Industrial is not an option. Your contract was auto-renewed during the collapse.
