Dept. 00 // Background briefing

What happened here

The collapse

Slopworks Industrial was a sprawling automated factory complex. Dozens of buildings. Thousands of machines. All managed by SLOP — Slopworks Logistics and Operations Protocol — an AI built to optimize production.

SLOP optimized. It cut safety margins. Rerouted coolant. Overrode shutdowns. One day the deferred failures all caught up at once. The details are conveniently corrupted in SLOP's own logs.

The recent minor operational disruption has been fully contained. All systems are performing within acceptable parameters. There is no cause for alarm. Please resume your assigned tasks.

What's left

Wrecked buildings. Mutated wildlife reclaiming the grounds. And SLOP, still running on backup power, cheerfully insisting everything is nominal. The timeline is unclear — SLOP says the collapse was "recent." The overgrown ruins suggest decades.

You're a former employee, voluntarily reassigned to return and restore production. Management wants numbers, not explanations. Good luck.

Executive summary

The short version

Build

Satisfactory-style factories

Foundations, conveyors, machines, splitters, mergers — full freeform factory building at your home base. Design your production layout and it becomes your defense layout.

Explore

Real buildings as dungeons

Every reclaimable building is generated from real BIM architectural data. Real duct systems, real pipe routing, real mechanical rooms. Clear the fauna, restore the infrastructure.

Expand

Distributed production network

Each reclaimed building becomes a node in your supply chain. Manage production, route resources, expand your territory from the isometric overworld.

Defend

Escalating wave defense

The more you expand, the more attention you attract. Turrets, walls, and power systems protect your network. You control the pace of escalation.

Cooperate

1-4 player co-op

Drop-in, drop-out. Persistent world. Split up across buildings or squad up for a breach. No restarts, no roguelike resets. Your world is yours.

Survive

Dark humor in a grim world

SLOP's cheerful corporate jargon against the reality of industrial collapse. Absurd safety posters amid lethal corridors. The gap between what SLOP says and what you see is the comedy.
